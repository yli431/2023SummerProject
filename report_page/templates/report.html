Introduction

This project aimed to determine the affordability of house prices in Christchurch and create a database of related data that may affect the real estate market.
The data collected and analyzed were presented on a website, where a chat robot answered questions about the database and performed a simple analysis.
Various charts were used to show possible relationships between the dataset and property values, such as family income, mortgage rates, and rental growth.
Thus, the housing affordability in Christchurch could be inferred.

Background

After experiencing a market that the pandemic has shaken, Christchurch remains the most affordable city among the top three big cities in New Zealand, especially for first-home buyers. The average house prices started increasing after 2020, peaked in 2022, then fell 17.8%, and appeared to bottom out in May 2023. Since bottoming out, NZ house prices have increased by 3.76%.
The average price of a Christchurch property is $754,858(CoreLogic, December 2023), which is 2.82% up over the last 12 months. Christchurch house prices soared after COVID-19; however, after that peak in February 2022, house prices fell 10.72% before bottoming out in June 2023. Since then, they have risen 4.43% till today.
Rents have been steadily increasing in Christchurch. Over the last four years (2019-2023), the median rent has gone up $130 a week, which is 7.46% a year on average. Mortgage rates kept increasing after 2022 to control the significant inflation in NZ. It has already shown a positive impact on CPI figures from the end of 2023, though it has brought pressure to families with higher mortgage loans.
